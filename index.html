Period Poverty

Period poverty is the lack of access to sanitary products, menstrual hygiene education, toilets, handwashing facilities, or waste management. There are 1.7 billion people that live without basic sanitation services. This makes it difficult for menstruating women to take necessary care of their periods.

In the education system teenage girls starting their menstruation often never have access to sanitary products. Often times these students will opt to stay at home rather, than face being teased by fellow classmates. This site serves to de-stigmatize menstruation and promote period health.

The Menstrual Cycle

The average length of a menstrual cycle is 28 days. However, a cycle can range in length from 21 days to about 35 days and still be normal.
