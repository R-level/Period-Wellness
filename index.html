Dysmenorrhea

  • Dysmenorrhea is characterized by severe and frequent menstrual cramps and pain during your period. may be primary, existing from the beginning of periods, or secondary, due to an underlying condition.
  • may include cramping or pain in the lower abdomen, low back pain, pain spreading down the legs, nausea, vomiting, diarrhea, fatigue, weakness, fainting, or headaches.
  • may include NSAIDS, acetaminophen, birth control pills, hormone treatment, dietary changes, vitamins, exercise, heat, or massage.
  • In extreme conditions, surgery may be needed.
